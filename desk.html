Rev Fr. Dr. Paul Thekkiniyath CMI,Principal

BE THE REASON FOR OTHERS’ SMILE

Lisieux, as a faith-based school, know that it is our duty to teach our students the importance of giving back and that means participation in community service. By instilling the importance of school, family and community, we are working to ensure that our students become active members in their communities. It is especially important to teach children that they are part of a larger community and that we are all responsible for those around us. By giving the values of social commitment, a central role in our school along with excellence in education, we are encouraging our children to grow up with a healthy sense of compassion and a strong charitable spirit. Whatever be the level of success our children achieve, they should uphold higher values rooted in compassion and love towards fellow beings. Being a student, they too should learn to make a difference by taking baby steps to support different social causes like creating awareness for the importance of education, finding shelter for the homeless, looking after the sick people, spending time with old age people, environment cleaning and so on. The experience of giving your time to support a cause will enrich yourself. It helps to create a strong bonding with their family members, teach the invaluable life skills of empathy, humility, patience, kindness and a plethora of other experiences that will help them as they navigate their future. Learning about giving and helping others gives children a feeling of empowerment. So we at Lisieux introduced LITES (Lisieux In Transcending and Empowering Students) with the motto of ‘Lighting Up Lives’ with an intention of inculcating the sense of social commitment and imparting human values in the young minds. This innovative and dynamic framework of holistic learning is designed to meet the changing needs of growing children and their evolving personalities. This well-structured, integrative approach exposes students to the teachings of Mahatma Gandhi who said, “The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others.” The best feeling of happiness is knowing you're the reason for others’ smile.
